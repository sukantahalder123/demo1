Swami Vivekananda, originally named Narendranath Datta, was born on January 12, 1863, in Calcutta (now Kolkata), India. Here is a brief overview of his childhood:

1. Family and Early Life:
Narendranath was born into a well-off family. His father, Vishwanath Datta, was an attorney, and his mother, Bhuvaneshwari Devi, was a devout woman. The family had a strong cultural and spiritual background.

2. Education:
Naren showed intellectual capabilities from an early age and was a good student. He excelled in various subjects and showed a keen interest in literature, music, and the arts.

3. Influence of Ramakrishna Paramahamsa:
Naren's life took a significant turn when he met Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, a mystic and spiritual leader. Initially skeptical, Naren eventually became one of Ramakrishna's most devoted disciples. The teachings of Ramakrishna had a profound impact on Naren's spiritual development.



As a child named Narendranath Datta, Swami Vivekananda was recognized for his mischievous and playful demeanor, spreading laughter and joy with his lively spirit and pranks. Despite a lack of interest in formal education, Naren's curious mind and quick wit distinguished him. His playful antics, such as practical jokes on friends and family, infused a sense of fun into the otherwise serious atmosphere of his surroundings.
